About Mahmoud Al-Khatib

Mahmoud Al-Khatib (born October 30, 1954) is a former Egyptian football player . He played for Al-Ahly Club and the Egyptian national football team and is the current president of the Egyptian Al-Ahly Club . Al-Khatib was born in an Egyptian village located in the Sinbillawain Center in Dakahlia Governorate . From the village, the little boy moved with his family to Cairo . Mahmoud Al-Khatib joined Al-Nasr Club in Heliopolis , and when he reached the age of fifteen, his name became frequent in the field of football, especially during his participation in the schools league with the sports school. Al-Nasr Club officials agreed to transfer Al-Khatib to Al-Ahly Club after he was close to signing for Al-Ismaily , and Al-Khatib was only 16 years old at the time. At this age, Al-Khatib played with the U-18 team, and his first match was against his former club, Al-Nasr. He rose to the first team to play his first official match against the Plastic Club on October 15, 1972. In the same match, he scored his first goal, and after that, the goals continued in the league until, on the day of his retirement, he reached 108 goals in the league championship, which he scored in 199 matches during 17 continuous years of playing. . on Twitter: @paultrani
